SAMMAL Product Page

REAKTOR's Pre - Assignment
Introduction
In July 2021 I participated in REAKTOR's Visual Design Pre-Assignment in order to apply for the Junior Designer position at the company.

The project was conducted remotely within about 7 hours in total. I divided the time into three slots: 1 hour for research and ideation, 4.5 hours for project execution, and the remaining for presentation.
Objectives
This pre-assignment asked the candidates to propose a visual concept for the product page of their fictional client - SAMMAL. The solution must include:
  • A chosen product - I chose a unisex white T-shirt
  • Product’s image, name, brand
  • Product description and material composition
  • Selection of size, colors
  • Price (is it on a discount or not? How does it look if it is?)
  • CTA (call to action) to buy the product
  • Ecometers (It could be Material reuse rate, Nearby production, Use of water in the supply chain, Recycling: reusing old models, Recycling possibilities (bring your old clothes here, etc.), or Collection releasing frequency)

SAMMAL web shop's visitors (The target user audience)
The designed product page functions as a channel to drive sales. That's why it should serve the right audience with the appropriate message and CTA that fit their concerns. Here are my assumptions about the target audience of SAMMAL webshop based on the given information from the pre-assignment.
AUDIENCE GROUP 1: REGULAR CUSTOMERS
  • SAMMAL's loyal customers mostly from Finland and the other Nordic nations
  • Their average income or higher (based on the income standard amongst the Nordic countries)
  • Age range: 25 - 50
  • These people care about the high quality and durability of the fabric.
  • Their style should be minimalistic and simple that matches the Nordic design style.
AUDIENCE GROUP 2: ENVIRONMENT ENTHUSIASTS
  • They are quite young and well-educated.
  • Age range: 20 - 30
  • These people care about the impact of the product materials and production process onto the environment.
AUDIENCE GROUP 3: THE INTERNATIONAL
  • These people are from big European cities such as Paris, London, Barcelona, Amsterdam, and Milan.
  • They are fond of Scandinavian design style and concerned about the environmental impact of the clothes they are wearing.
  • Additionally, urban American, Canadian, Japanese, or Korean youth could be the brand's potential customers.
Competitor analysis
The first hour of the project was used for researching how SAMMAL's possible competitors present their product on the website.
‍
Here are the purposes of this competitor analysis:
  • To build up the content structure for the final SAMMAL product page 
  • To brainstorm the visual style that resonates with the brand's target audience 
Criteria for choosing the competitors:
  • Fashion brands that promote sustainability and eco-friendliness.
  • These brands should either be from the Nordic countries or have products delivered to the Nordic markets. This criterion helps verify that their business is serving the same customer pool as SAMMAL.
  • International product shipment is available on their web shops.
  • Their products should be affordable to customers with an average or higher income. I evaluated the price range based on my knowledge about the individual average income from Finland.
COMPETITORS
1. MARIMEKKO
(Finland)
2. Nudie Jeans
(Sweden)
The way Nudie Jeans showcases how their product is created. By clicking those elements at the Transparency section, their visitors can read details about the raw materials, manufacture process, warehouse location, etc.
3. Organic Basics
(Denmark)
Customers will find out the reduced amount of carbon dioxide, chemicals, and waste the production of an Organic Basics Rib-Flex Tee can get in comparison to the corresponding result from the traditional process.

The Impact Index is located right below the product general information section. Big icons are also used besides the figures in order to capture the attention of the customers.
4. Reformation
(The USA)
The Reformation brand promotes sustainability in terms of working environment, circularity, and social consciousness towards the climate.

Since Reformation supports world-wide shipment, the way its content is organised on the web shop reflects how international customers would want to be resonated with the brand.
5. ZARA / H&M
As for the mobile version, web content is usually organised vertically.

A fixed box that stays persistently at the bottom of the screen presents options of colour and quantity and a buying button. Customers can both scroll down to read more information about the product and proceed with the purchasing process at any time.
Ideation
Since there was limited time to create the solution to this task, a UI layout of the product page was given in advance. The candidate could rely on this layout to brainstorm ideas and add more details to the outcome.
CONTENT STRUCTURE
The content structure of SAMMAL's product page has five major sections including the navigation bar, the main product area, other product suggestion area, email subscription banner, and the footer as the result from the competitor analysis above.
