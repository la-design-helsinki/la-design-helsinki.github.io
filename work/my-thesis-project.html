My Bachelor's Thesis Project

E-guide cover designs
It was a nice memory of the youth.
Over one year ago, deciding to quit the current job left me with tons of questions about my career path and the completely uncertain future ahead. What would be the next move then? I did not know, to be honest. Try something brand-new? Well then you have no idea about the inner fear of stepping the hell out of my comfort zone.

My zone was comfy indeed. I could not see any legit reasons to break through it. The funny thing was that, to forget the mess and others' voices, I ended up getting back to drawing which had been the holy centre of my comfort zone for a long time. I thought sometimes the best solution to a problem was to avoid it.

The early sketch of Sleeping Rabbit was on paper. I painted it with for-beginner (I mean, "cheap-ass") markers. The painting would have done until I bumped into a Wacom Cintiq at Oodi Library (in Helsinki). The tool looked whoppingly awesome, and I got hooked. The first day was to explore it. I found a huge amount of tutorials on YouTube. The second day was about struggling with layers and compositions on Pts. The third, fourth and many other days followed until the job was finished.

And you see it now! So proud of it (smiley smiley). I was a bit surprised when realizing that I had just tried on a new tool to an old thingy. It was out of my comfort zone, wasn't it? Or crossing into a wider one? You guys may tell.
